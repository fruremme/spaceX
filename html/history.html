History of space exploration

Elon Musk plan to colonize mars and to make humans a multiplanetary species

Take a look at historical events that lead to this opportunity

1610

Galileo Galilei

did the first telescopic observation of the night sky

1840

John William Draper

took the very first photograph of the Moon

1895

Konstantin Tsiolkovsky

proposed a type of planet-to-space transportation system. "The space elevator"

1926

Robert H. Goddard

invented the first liquid-fuel rocket in 1914, and successfully launched his rocket on March 16, 1926

1947

First animals in space were fruit flies

A rhesus monkey named Albert became the first monkey in space June 1949. He died of suffocation during the flight

1950

Bumper 8 - First rocket launch from Cape Canaveral

Low-angle atmospheric flight over 320 km range

1956

Jupiter-C - First rocket to pass the Thermopause and enter the Exosphere

At 682 miles (1,097.57 km) altitude and 3,335 miles range

1957

Sputnik1 - First signals from space

It was launched as part of the Soviet space program and was the first artificial Earth satellite

It orbited for three weeks before its batteries died and then orbited silently for two months before it fell back into the atmosphere on 4 January 1958, and burned up

1957

Sputnik2 - First dog in space

The first rocket to carry a living animal into earth orbit, a Soviet space dog named Laika. Laika died the next day due to overheating

1959

First monkey to make it back from space alive

1961

Vostok 1 - First human in space

Yuri Gagarin was a Soviet pilot and cosmonaut who became the first human in outer space, achieving a major milestone in the Space Race.

The flight took 108 minutes from launch to landing

1961

First United States human spaceflight - Piloted by astronaut Alan Shepard

Mercury-Redstone 3 (Freedom 7). The flight lasted 15 minutes, 22 seconds and the spacecraft traveled 302 miles (486 km)

1963

Vostok 6 - First woman in space

Valentina Tereshkova was a soviet cosmonaut and her solo flight lasted 88 hours, 28 minutes. She landed safely by parachute.

1969

Neil Armstrong (NASA) - Became the first man to walk on the Moon
